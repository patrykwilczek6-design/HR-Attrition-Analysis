HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard
Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition patterns using the IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance dataset. The goal was to identify key reasons why employees leave the company and provide actionable recommendations to reduce turnover.
Dataset

Source: IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition & Performance (Kaggle)
Size: 1,470 employees
Features: Age, Department, Job Role, Monthly Income, Years at Company, Attrition, and more

Key Findings

Overall attrition rate: 16% (237 out of 1,470 employees)
Highest attrition department: Research & Development (133 employees left)
Highest attrition role: Laboratory Technician (62 out of 133 in R&D)
Employees who left earned on average $2,920/month vs $3,340/month for those who stayed
Most attrition occurs within the first 2 years of employment

Recommendations

Raise the lower salary bracket for Laboratory Technicians
Increase qualification requirements to build a more skilled workforce
Implement retention programs for employees in their first 2 years
